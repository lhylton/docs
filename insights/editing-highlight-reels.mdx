After creating a highlight reel, you may want to fine-tune it before sharing — adding more context to a clip that feels too brief, or moving a particularly strong participant moment to the front. Strella gives you two editing tools: adjusting the length of individual clips directly in the transcript, and reordering clips by dragging the notes panel.

## Edit 1 — Adjust the length of a clip

Use this when a clip feels like it's missing context or starts/ends at the wrong moment.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the insight in the Saved tab">
    Go to the **Saved tab** and click the insight whose reel you want to edit. The highlight reel player and its associated clips will load on the page.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'View session' on the clip you want to adjust">
    Find the clip in the right-hand panel and click **View session**. Strella takes you directly to the point in the original session transcript where that clip was captured.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag the start or end bar to resize the selection">
    In the transcript, you'll see a **blue start bar** and a **blue end bar** marking the boundaries of your current selection. Click and drag either bar to expand or shrink the clip — move them outward to include more context, or inward to tighten the focus.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Return to the Saved tab">
    Navigate back to the Saved tab. The highlight reel will reflect your updated clip boundaries.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  You can adjust the clip boundaries as many times as you need. Each change is saved immediately — there's no separate save button.
</Note>

---

## Edit 2 — Reorder clips in the reel

Use this when you want a specific participant moment to appear first — for example, the clearest or most impactful quote.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the insight in the Saved tab">
    Go to the **Saved tab** and click the insight you want to reorder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Locate the notes panel on the right side of the page">
    The right panel lists each clip as a note, in the same order they appear in the highlight reel. The top note plays first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Drag and drop notes into the order you want">
    Click and hold a note, then drag it up or down in the list to reposition it. Release it in the new position.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the reel order has updated">
    The highlight reel player reflects the new order immediately. Play through the reel to confirm the sequence tells the story you want.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Lead with the most persuasive quote. Stakeholders who only watch the first clip are far more likely to engage with the rest of the reel if the opening moment is immediately relevant.
</Tip>
