Getting insights in front of the right people shouldn't require everyone to have a Strella account. You can share any highlight reel using a shareable link that works for anyone, or download the reel as a video file for use in presentations or async communication tools.

<Info>
  Both sharing methods are available for any insight in your Saved tab. Shareable links do not require the recipient to log in or create a Strella account.
</Info>

## Method 1 — Share via a link

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the insight in the Saved tab">
    Navigate to the **Saved tab** and click the insight you want to share.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the highlight reel menu">
    In the top-right corner of the highlight reel player, click the **3 dots (⋯)** menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the shareable link">
    Click **Create shareable link**. Strella generates a unique public URL for this reel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Copy the link to your clipboard">
    Click **Copy to clipboard**, then paste the link wherever you'd like to share it — Slack, email, a Notion doc, or anywhere else your team works.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The shareable link grants view-only access to the highlight reel. Recipients cannot edit the reel, access other insights, or view any other part of your Strella project.
</Note>

---

## Method 2 — Download the reel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the insight in the Saved tab">
    Navigate to the **Saved tab** and click the insight you want to download.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open the highlight reel menu">
    In the top-right corner of the highlight reel player, click the **3 dots (⋯)** menu.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click 'Download Reel'">
    Select **Download Reel**. Strella begins processing the video in the background — this may take a few minutes depending on the length of the reel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your email for the download link">
    When the reel has finished processing, you'll receive an email containing a link to download the video file. Click the link to save the reel to your device.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use the download option when you're embedding a clip in a presentation deck, submitting it to a stakeholder repository, or sharing it in a tool that doesn't support external links well.
</Tip>
